# Drift — Stop Letting Things Silently Slip Away

## The Problem

People don't lack ambition — they lack follow-through.

Everyone has a list of things they care about: finishing a book, filing taxes on time, getting back into running, building a side project. They start with energy, but life gets in the way. Slowly, silently, things get abandoned.

No tool catches this. To-do apps store tasks but don't care if you ignore them. Calendars manage time but don't know what matters to you. Habit trackers count streaks but don't understand your bigger picture.

The result: a quiet pile of half-finished intentions and a lingering feeling of "I should be doing more."

## The Insight

The problem isn't planning. It's sustained attention over time.

People don't need another list. They need something that:
- Remembers what they committed to
- Notices when things start drifting
- Helps them decide — consciously — what to continue, pause, or let go

## The Solution

An AI-powered personal clarity companion that turns mental chaos into focused action — and keeps you honest over weeks and months, not just today.

### How It Works

**1. Brain Dump — Say whatever's on your mind**
No forms, no categories. Just talk (text or voice). Tasks, goals, half-baked ideas, obligations — dump it all. The AI understands context, extracts what matters, and organizes it for you.

**2. Set Your Lens — What would make this month a win?**
You tell the app what success looks like at different time horizons (this week, this month, this quarter). This becomes the filter for everything else.

**3. Daily Clarity — Two things, not twenty**
Every morning, the app tells you the one or two things that matter most today — based on your goals, deadlines, and what's been slipping. It makes the decision so you don't have to.

**4. Drift Detection — Nothing silently dies**
If you haven't touched something in a while, the app notices. It nudges you — not as a nag, but as a thoughtful prompt: "You said this mattered. Still true?" It breaks stale items into tiny next steps to reduce friction.

**5. Weekly Mirror — See the truth, without judgment**
A simple weekly reflection: here's what you said mattered, here's what you actually did. No guilt — just a factual mirror that helps you recalibrate.

**6. Conscious Abandonment — Choose to stop, don't just forget**
When something has been ignored long enough, the app asks you to make a choice: recommit, park it for later, or let it go. This is the key difference — things end intentionally, not by neglect.

## What Makes This Different

| Existing Tools | Drift |
|---|---|
| Organize tasks for today | Tracks commitments over weeks and months |
| You decide what to do | AI decides what matters most right now |
| Silent when you stop | Notices drift and nudges before things die |
| Lists grow endlessly | Forces conscious choices: continue, pause, or let go |
| Input requires structure | Accepts messy, unstructured brain dumps |

## The Experience in One Line

It's like having a thoughtful friend with perfect memory and zero judgment.

## Who Is This For

- People who start things but struggle to finish them
- Anyone who feels overwhelmed by competing priorities
- People whose important-but-not-urgent tasks (taxes, health, learning) always get pushed
- Anyone who wants clarity on what actually deserves their attention

## Market Context

Existing products like TaskMelt, Motion, and todo.ai focus on organizing and scheduling tasks for today. Habit trackers like Habitica and Streaks count daily repetitions. None of them solve the deeper problem: sustained follow-through on meaningful commitments over time, and the conscious management of what you choose to pursue or let go.
